LAWS OF THE REPUBLIC OF INDONESIA

NUMBER 62 YEAR 1958

ON

CITIZENSHIP OF THE REPUBLIC OF INDONESIA (LNRI 1958/113)

The President of the Republic of Indonesia,

Deliberates:

The necessity of promulgating a citizenship law of the Republic of Indonesia;

Considering:

a.Articles 5 and 144 of the Provisional Constitution of the Republic of Indonesia;

b.Article 89 of the Provisional Constitution of the Republic of Indonesia

With the approval of the House of Representatives;

DECIDES:

To stipulate:

To enact Laws on Citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia.

ARTICLE 1

Citizens of the Republic of Indonesia are:

a.People who are, based on the laws and/or agreements and/or regulations enforced since the Proclamation of 17th August 1945, already citizens of the Republic of Indonesia;

b.A person who at birth has a legal family relationship with the father, a citizen of the Republic of Indonesia, with the understanding that the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia starts from the existence of said family legal relationship, and that this family legal relationship is established before the person is 18 years old or before marrying under 18 years of age;

c.A child born within 300 days after the father’s death, should the father at the time of death is a citizen of the Republic of Indonesia;

d.A person whose mother is a citizen of the Republic of Indonesia at the time of birth, should the person not have a legal family relationship with his father;

e.A person whose mother is a citizen of the Republic of Indonesia at the time of birth, should the father does not have citizenship, or as long as the nationality of the father is unknown;

f.A person born within the territory of the Republic of Indonesia should both parents are unknown;

g.A child found within the territory of the Republic of Indonesia should both parents are unknown;

h.A person born within the territory of the Republic of Indonesia, should both parents do not have citizenship or should the citizenships of both parents are unknown;

i.A person born within the territory of the Republic of Indonesia who at the time of birth did not have the citizenship of the father or mother, and should the person did not have the citizenship of his father or mother;

j.A person who acquires the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia according to the provisions of this Law.

ARTICLE 2

(1)A foreign child who has not reached the age of 5 years and adopted by a citizen of the Republic of Indonesia, acquires the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia, if the appointment is declared valid by the District Court from the place of residence of the person who adopted the child.

(2)The said legal statement by the District Court must be requested by the person who adopted the child within 1 year after the adoption, or within 1 year after this Law is enforced.

ARTICLE 3

(1)A child out of wedlock from a mother who is a citizen of the Republic of Indonesia or a child from a legal marriage, but handed over by a judge in a divorce to the care of his mother, a citizen of the Republic of Indonesia, whose citizenship is shared by a foreign father, may submit an application to the Minister of Justice to obtain citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia, should after obtaining the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia the child does not have another nationality, or submits a statement of renunciation of other citizenships in the manner determined by the legal provisions of the child‚Äôs country of origin and/or in the manner determined by the settlement agreement of dual citizenships between the Republic of Indonesia and the country concerned.

(2)The above-mentioned application must be submitted within 1 year after the person concerned is 18 years of age to the Minister of Justice through the District Court or the Representative of the Republic of Indonesia from his place of residence.

(3)The Minister of Justice grants or rejects the application with the approval of the Council of Ministers.

(4)The citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia obtained on the application is enforced on the date of the decision by the Minister of Justice.

ARTICLE 4

(1)A foreigner who is born and resides in the Territory of the Republic of Indonesia whose father or mother, if the person does not have a family relationship with his father, was also born within the territory of the Republic of Indonesia, may submit an application to the Minister of Justice to obtain citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia, should after obtaining the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia, the person does not have another nationality, or at the time of submitting the application also submits a statement of renunciation of other citizenships that the person may have in accordance with the legal provisions enforced by person‚Äôs country of origin or in accordance with the provisions in the settlement agreement of dual citizenships between the Republic of Indonesia and the country concerned.

(2)The above-mentioned application must be submitted within 1 year after the person concerned is 18 years old to the Minister of Justice through the District Court of his place of residence.

(3)The Minister of Justice grants or rejects the application with the approval of the Council of Ministers.

(4)The citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia obtained on the application shall be enforced on the date of the decision of the Minister of Justice

ARTICLE 5

(1)Citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia because citizenship is obtained by the enactment of the decision of the Minister of Justice that grants citizenship.

(2)To apply for citizenship, the applicant must:

a.Be already 21 years old;

b.Born in the territory of the Republic of Indonesia, or at the time of application, have resided in that area for at least 5 consecutive years most recently, or for 10 non-consecutive years;

c.If the person is a married man, he gets the approval of his wife (or wives).

d.Sufficiently able to speak Indonesian language, and has satisfactory knowledge of Indonesian history, and has never been convicted of a crime that is detrimental to the Republic of Indonesia;

e.In good mental and physical health;

f.Pay the state treasury in the amount of between Rp. 500 to Rp. 10,000 which is determined by the Tax Office of the person‚Äôs residency, based on real monthly income provided which cannot exceed monthly real income,

g.Has a steady livelihood;

h.Does not have citizenship, or will lose citizenship if the person acquires the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia, or submits a statement of renunciation of other citizenships according to the legal provisions of country of origin or according to the legal provisions of the settlement agreement on dual citizenships between the Republic of Indonesia and the country concerned. A woman during marriage may not apply for citizenship.

(3)The application for citizenship must be submitted in writing and affixed with a stamp duty to the Minister of Justice through the District Court or Representative of the Republic of Indonesia from the place of residence of the applicant; The application must be written in the Indonesian language and together with the application the evidence regarding the matters referred to in paragraph 2 except those referred to in paragraph (d) must be submitted. The District Court or Representative of the Republic of Indonesia shall provide such evidence as to its veracity and test the applicant for his proficiency in the Indonesian language and knowledge of Indonesian history.

(4)The Minister of Justice grants or rejects the application for citizenship with the approval of the Council of Ministers.

(5)The decision of the Minister of Justice granting citizenship shall be enforced on the day the applicant appears before the District Court or Representative of the Republic of Indonesia from his place of residence to take an oath or pledge of allegiance and apply retroactively until the date of the decision of the Minister of Justice.

The oath or pledge of allegiance is as follows:

“I swear (promise);

that I completely renounce all allegiance to foreign powers;

that I recognize and accept the supreme authority of the Republic of Indonesia and will remain loyal to it;

that I will uphold the Constitution and the laws of the Republic Indonesia

and will defend it earnestly;

That I carry this obligation willingly and will not reduce it in the slightest. ”

(6)After the applicant has taken the oath or pledge of allegiance as referred to above, the Minister of Justice shall announce the citizenship by placing his decision in the State Gazette.

(7)If the oath or pledge of allegiance is not pronounced within three months after the date of the decision of the Minister of Justice, the decision will automatically be invalidated.

(8)The amount referred to in paragraph 2 is repaid, if the application for citizenship is not granted.

(9)If the application for citizenship is rejected, the applicant can reapply.

ARTICLE 6

Citizenship can also be granted on the grounds of the interest of the State or services to the State by the Government with the approval of the House of Representatives. In this case and the provisions in article 5 only apply the provisions of paragraph 1, paragraph 5, paragraph 6 and paragraph 7.

ARTICLE 7

(1)A foreign woman who marries a citizen of the Republic of Indonesia, acquires the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia, if and at the time, within 1 year after the marriage takes place, states a statement for that purpose, except if she acquires the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia, she still has another citizenship, in the case of which information cannot be stated.

(2)With the exception referred to in paragraph 1, a foreign woman who marries a citizen of the Republic of Indonesia also acquires the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia one year after her marriage takes place, if within that one year her husband does not provide a statement to renounce her citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia. The statement can only be stated and only results in the loss of the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia, if by losing it, the husband does not become stateless.

(3)If one of the statements in paragraphs 1 and 2 has been stated, the other information may not be stated.

(4)The above information must be stated to the District Court or the Indonesian State Representative from the place of residence of the person stating the information.

ARTICLE 8

(1)A woman who is a citizen of the Republic of Indonesia who marries a foreigner loses her citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia, if and within 1 year after her marriage takes place, she declares a statement for that purpose, except if she loses her citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia and becomes stateless.

(2)The information referred to in paragraph 1 must be stated to the District Court or the Representative of the Republic of Indonesia from the place of residence of the person stating the information for that purpose.

ARTICLE 9

(1)The citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia obtained by a husband shall automatically apply to his wife, except if after obtaining the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia the wife still has another citizenship.

(2)Loss of citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia by a husband shall automatically apply to his wife, unless the wife will become stateless.

ARTICLE 10

(1)A woman in her marriage may not submit the application mentioned in article 3 and article 4.

(2)Loss of citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia by a wife automatically applies to her husband, unless the husband will become stateless.

ARTICLE 11

(1)A person who is caused by or as a result of his marriage loses the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia, regains that citizenship if and at the time after his marriage is terminated he provides a statement for that. The statement must be stated within 1 year after the marriage is terminated to the District Court or the Representative of the Republic of Indonesia from the place of residence.

(2)The provisions of paragraph 1 shall not apply in the case of that person if after regaining the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia, he still has another nationality.

ARTICLE 12

(1)A woman who is caused by or as a result of her marriage acquires the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia, loses that citizenship again, if and at the time, after her marriage is terminated, states a statement for that.

The statement must be stated within 1 year after the marriage is terminated to the District Court or the Representative of the Republic of Indonesia from the place of residence.

(2)The provisions of paragraph 1 shall not apply if the person loses the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia and becomes stateless.

ARTICLE 13

(1)A child who is under 18 years of age and has never been married who has a legal family relationship with his father before the father obtained the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia, also acquires the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia after he resides and is in Indonesia. The provisions concerning domicile and being in Indonesia do not apply to children who because their father have obtained the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia become stateless.

(2)Citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia obtained by a mother also applies to her children who do not have a legal family relationship with their father, who are not yet 18 years old and have not married after they have resided and are in Indonesia. If the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia is obtained by citizenship by a mother who has become a widow because her husband died, then children who have a legal family relationship with the husband, who are not yet 18 years old and not yet married, also acquire the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia, after they live in and reside in Indonesia. The provision regarding residence in Indonesia does not apply to children who because their mother has obtained the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia become stateless.

ARTICLE 14

If the child referred to in article 2 and article 13 reaches the age of 21 years, then he loses the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia again, if and at the time he makes a statement for that.

The statement must be stated within 1 year after the child turns 21 years old to the District Court or the Representative of the Republic of Indonesia from his place of residence.

The provisions of paragraph 1 do not apply if the child by losing the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia becomes stateless.

ARTICLE 15

Loss of citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia by a father applies to his children who have a legal family relationship with the father, who are not yet 18 years old and not yet married, unless by losing their citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia the children become stateless.

The loss of citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia by a mother also applies to her children who do not have a legal family relationship with their father, except if by losing the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia the children become stateless.

If the mother loses the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia due to citizenship abroad and the mother has become a widow because her husband died, then the provisions in paragraph 2 shall also apply to her children who have a legal family relationship with the husband, after the children are located and be abroad.

ARTICLE 16

(1)A child who loses the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia because his father or mother has lost that nationality, regains the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia after the child reaches the age of 18 years, if at the time he gives a statement for that.

The said information must be stated within 1 year after the child turns 18 to the District Court or Representative of the Republic of Indonesia from his place of residence.

(2)The statement in paragraph 1 does not apply in the event that the child, if after obtaining the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia, still has another nationality.

ARTICLE 17

Indonesian citizenship is lost due to:

a.Acquiring another citizenship of his own free will with the understanding that if the person concerned at the time of obtaining another nationality is in the territory of the Republic of Indonesia, his citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia will only be deemed lost if the Minister of Justice with the approval of the Council of Ministers of his own free will or at the request of the person concerned declares that he is missing;

b.Not refusing or giving up another nationality, while the person concerned has the opportunity to do so;

c.Recognized by a foreigner as his/her child, if the person concerned is not yet 18 years old and unmarried and by losing the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia does not become stateless;

d.A child who is adopted legally by a foreigner as his child, if the child concerned is not yet 5 years old and by losing the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia does not become stateless;

e.Declared missing by the Minister of Justice with the approval of the Council of Ministers at the request of the person concerned, if he is 21 years of age, resides abroad and is declared to have lost his Indonesian citizenship, he does not become stateless;

f.Entering the service of a foreign army without prior permission from the Minister of Justice;

g.Without prior permission from the Minister of Justice to enter the service of a foreign country or the service of an inter-state organization which the Republic of Indonesia does not enter as a member, if the state service position held by him according to the regulations of the Republic of Indonesia can only be held by a citizen or a position in the service of the said interstate organization require an oath or promise of office;

h.Taking an oath or pledge allegiance to a foreign country or part of it; i. without being obliged to participate in the election of something of a constitutional nature for a foreign country,

i.Having a passport or a letter that is a passport from a foreign country in his name which is still valid;

j.Other than for state service, for 5 consecutive years residing abroad without expressing his desire to remain a citizen before that time has passed and so on every two years; the desire must be expressed to the Representative of the Republic of Indonesia from his place of residence. For a citizen of the Republic of Indonesia, who is under the age of 18, unless he has been married before, the five-year period mentioned above shall come into effect on the day he reaches the age of 18.

ARTICLE 18

A person who loses his citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia as referred to in Article 17 paragraph k shall regain his citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia if he resides in Indonesia based on an Entry Permit Card and provides information for that. The statement must be stated to the District Court from the place of residence and 1 year after the person, is domiciled in Indonesia.

ARTICLE 19

Citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia given or obtained based on incorrect information may be revoked by the agency that gave it or by the agency that received the information.

ARTICLE 20

Whoever is not an Indonesian citizen of the Republic of Indonesia is a foreigner.

SUMMARY EXPLANATION

OF

LAW ON CITIZENSHIP FOR THE REPUBLIC OF INDONESIA

I.GENERAL

The citizenship law in essence regulates:

I.Acquiring citizenship

II.Losing citizenship and obtaining citizenship

According to this law, the Citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia is obtained through:

a.Birth

b.Adoption

c.Granted application

d.Citizenship

e.By or as a result of marriage

f.Joining father/mother

g.Written statement

A.Birth

In this law, citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia is obtained because of birth based on descent and based on birth within the territory of the Republic of Indonesia to prevent people from being stateless.

That heredity is used as a basis is common. It is natural for a country to consider a child as its citizen wherever he is born, if the child’s parents are citizens of that country.

At the same time, the child’s parents do not always have the same citizenship, and not always the child has a legal family relationship with his parents. Therefore, one of the parents must take precedence.

In terms of citizenship, this law assumes that there is always a legal family relationship between the child and the mother; The legal family relationship between the child and the father only exists if the child is born in or from a legal marriage or if the child is legally recognized by the father.

If there is a family law relationship between the child and the father, then the father determines the nationality of the child (see Article 1 paragraphs b and c), unless the father is unable to

determine the nationality of his child because he does not have citizenship or because his nationality is unknown, in which case the mother decides (see Article 1 paragraph e).

If there is no familial legal relationship between the father and the father, the one who determines the nationality of the child is the mother (see Article 1 paragraph 1).

Birth within the territory of the Republic of Indonesia as a basis for obtaining citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia in this law is only used to prevent the existence of stateless persons born within the territory of the Republic of Indonesia and is only used as long as necessary to avoid that (see Article 1 paragraph f, g, and h).

B.Adoption

Adoption of children is common in Indonesia. The legal adoption of a child is determined by the law of adopting a child. Sometimes the adopted child is a foreigner, but because he is truly treated as his own child, the origin of that person is no longer known or felt. So, such a child should be given the status of the adoptive parent.

As a guarantee that the adoption is truly an adoption as described above and so that the foreign child who is adopted can really still feel like an Indonesian citizen, the granting of Indonesian citizenship to the adopted child should be limited to a very young child (see Article 2).

C.Granted Application

1.There is a possibility that a child may be due to the enactment of a rule to take into account the citizenship of his father, while in fact he feels closer to his mother, who is a citizen of the Republic of Indonesia. The child should be given the opportunity to obtain the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia, if he is considered to have been able to determine his own nationality. The granting of this opportunity should be limited to children outside of marriage, because in marriage, parents and children are principally a unit whose status is determined by the father. Meanwhile, because the person concerned has been a foreigner for so long, the opportunity is in the form of a request.

On obtaining citizenship by application, see Article 3.

2.Countries that allow people from outside to settle in its territory, at some point in time should accept descendants from outsiders in its citizenship environment. The extent to which and the manner in which us soli (right of soil or birthright citizenship) is carried out on people who are not stateless depends on the circumstances of each country. Because citizenship should not be enforced on people who already have other nationalities, entry into the Indonesian citizenship environment should come from the person’s own wishes. For reasons as above (no. 1), the opportunity given is in the form of a request. According to this law, the people who are given this opportunity are those who were born from a resident or who later became residents, who were also born in Indonesia.

A further condition is that he or she does not become a national excess (see Article 4).

D.Citizenship

A foreigner who really wants to become a citizen of the Republic of Indonesia should be given the opportunity to carry out that desire. Of course, Indonesia’s interests should not be disturbed by the granting of citizenship. So that the granting of citizenship does not conflict with the purpose of the granting, then conditions are held, all of which are objective.

Because the granting of citizenship includes the discretion of the executive power, the one who grants citizenship is the Government, in this case the Minister of Justice with the approval of the Council of Ministers.

Of course, the Government in terms of granting citizenship is responsible to the Parliament, and must not deviate from the specified conditions. Regarding the matter of citizenship, furthermore, please read Article 5 which is clear enough. It is ordinary citizenship at the request of people who wish to become citizens of the Republic of Indonesia.

There is a possibility that for Indonesia’s own interests it is necessary to have a citizen, or a foreigner, because he has served the Republic of Indonesia as if he were a citizen. In this case, the conditions specified for the application for ordinary citizenship do not apply.

See Article 6.

E.By or As a Result of Marriage

This law holds that in marriage both the bride and groom should have the same citizenship as far as possible. If this will result in an excess of citizenship or without citizenship or eliminate the citizenship of a person who is considered to be heavy, then the principle of nationality unity is released.

The matter of marriage, which is also related to the issue of loss of citizenship, will be explained further below.

F.Joining Father/Mother

In essence, children who are not yet adults also get Republican citizenship of Indonesia with father or mother, if there is no family law relationship with his father. The position of the child will be determined further below.

G.Written Statement

Apart from a foreign woman who marries a citizen of the Republic of Indonesia to obtain the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia more than one year after the marriage takes place (article 7 paragraph I) and to people to regain the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia who is lost due to participation in another person, other This law only provides the possibility to obtain citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia with a statement to people who, in connection with the transitional situation where there is a vacuum in the citizenship regulations of the Republic of Indonesia, cannot become citizens of the Republic of Indonesia (see transitional regulations articles III, IV , V, VI).

II. Loss of citizenship

Apart from the consequences of marriage and father/mother participation, which will be explained below, other things which causes the loss of citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia, in this law stated in article 17.

The loss of citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia can be caused by the person in question obtaining a new citizenship of his own volition or because he wants to have citizenship only while he does not reside in Indonesia, or because of actions that can show that the person concerned does not or does not value citizenship. Republic of Indonesia.

At the same time, acquiring another nationality of its own accord does not necessarily result in the loss of citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia Article 17 paragraph a). Loss of citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia frees the person concerned from the obligations of being a citizen, on the other hand if the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia is not lost, then he cannot be treated as a foreigner. Therefore, the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia should only be lost if there is a statement from the Government (in this case the Minister of Justice with the approval of the Council of Ministers). The Minister of Justice stated that the disappearance was of his own free will or at the request of the person concerned.

From citizens residing abroad as stated in Article 17 paragraph k, assistance can be expected to periodically notify the Representatives of the Republic of Indonesia from their place of residence, that they are still citizens of the Republic of Indonesia and still wish to continue to be citizens. For citizens residing abroad due to carrying out state service, the statement is deemed unnecessary.

The sanction for the negligence of these people is indeed heavy, but the sanction can be easily removed when he returns to Indonesia (see Article 18).

Taking on Another Nationality

1.Marriage

As explained above, this law prioritizes the principle of the unity of citizenship of the bride and groom, which principle is not implemented if it results in excess citizenship or statelessness, or it is difficult to alienate a citizen who is married to a foreigner.

Basically it is the husband who determines the nationality of the husband. Since it is difficult to alienate a citizen because of her marriage, according to this law, a female citizen of the Republic of Indonesia, who marries a foreigner, does not lose her citizenship because of that marriage, unless she releases herself, and by releasing her she will not become a citizen. stateless.

Although basically the husband’s nationality determines, this law also provides an opportunity for male citizens to relinquish their citizenship, because it is possible only in this way to achieve unity of citizenship.

Due to the opportunities for men mentioned above and in connection with preventing the emergence of excess citizenship, a foreign woman who marries a citizen of the Republic of Indonesia, does not always obtain citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia.

She obtains her husband’s citizenship as soon as she gives a statement for it or - if within one year after the marriage in question takes place there is no statement from the woman, or there is no valid statement from her husband to renounce her citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia - one year after the marriage took place.

One and another with exceptions, if the woman after obtaining the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia still has another citizenship.

(Refer to Article 7 and Article 8).

The principle of national unity of the two brides subsequently turns out to be in terms of obtaining and losing the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia. (refer to Article 9 and Article 10).

2.Child position

In general, children who are immature‚Äîthat is, not yet 18 years old and unmarried‚Äîwith their father or mother, if there is no legal relationship with the father.

In one case the change in the status of a mother applies to all her children; that is, if the mother is widowed because her husband died and the change in status is due to an act that requires serious consideration, namely because of citizenship.

Because obtaining a new citizenship can be said to have a real meaning if the person is domiciled in the country that gave the new citizenship, the child will only acquire the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia after he is in Indonesia.

Due to the principle of not making people stateless, the child does not lose the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia with his/her father/mother, if thus he/she becomes stateless, and the conditions for being in Indonesia for the child mentioned above do not apply if the child becomes stateless.

Regarding the position of the child, see Article 13 and 15, as well as the closing regulations of Article III.

3.Back to origin

A person who changes citizenship due to being carried by another person or following another person in principle should be given the opportunity to return to his country of origin if that person is no longer with the other person.

So a woman who acquires the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia due to her husband’s participation, may in time relinquish the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia again, a woman who loses the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia due to the participation of her husband/wife may acquire the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia again.

The same is true for children who have their father or mother, or adopted children. The principle that prevents releasing the citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia is that the person concerned becomes stateless. The principle that prevents a person from obtaining citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia again is the principle of preventing excess citizenship.

On the matter of this return to origin, see Articles 11, 12, 14, and 16.

Miscellaneous

a.The legal relationship between a person and a country is not stated in terms of the same meaning and content in each country. So in this law it is explained that what is meant by citizenship is any kind of relationship with a country that results in the state’s obligation to protect the person concerned (see the closing regulation of Article II).

b.This law does not want the existence of excess citizenship, but this from one side cannot be prevented. PRESIDENTIAL DECREE OF THE REPUBLIC OF INDONESIA

NUMBER 10 YEAR 1959

The President of the Republic of Indonesia,

Deliberates:

a.That in order to carry out the Indonesianization of trade enterprises in general and the socialization of distribution apparatus in particular, in accordance with the national business development program and with the Cabinet Work program, deems necessary the establishment for regulations on foreign trade enterprises;

b.That concrete steps need to be taken towards political implementation, as outlined in the Presidential Mandate on the 14th anniversary of the Indonesian Proclamation of Independence on 17 August 1959, regarding the mobilization of progressive capital and manpower to be included in the field of development;

Considering:

1.Article 4 paragraph (1) of the Constitution;

2.Business Regulations 1934;

3.Government Regulation No. 1 of 1957;

4.Joint Decision Letter of the Minister of Industry and the Minister of Trade No. 2077/M/Perind.‚ÅÑ2430/M/Perdag. September 3, 1957;

5.Law No. 79 of 1958;

6.Decree of the Minister of Trade No. 2933/M dated 14 May 1959;

7.Government Announcement No. 1 dated 2 September 1959;

Hearing:

Cabinet Working Group on 3 November 1959;

DECIDES:

To stipulate:

Presidential Regulation on the prohibition of small-scale and foreign trade outside the capital Level I and II, along with Residency.

SECTION I:

DEFINITION OF FOREIGN SMALL/RETAIL TRADING COMPANY

Article 1

What is meant by “foreign small and retail trading companies” in this Presidential Decree are companies that are subject to prohibition based on the Decree of the Minister of Trade dated 14 May 1959 No. 2933/M, namely companies that:

1.Seek profits from the purchase and sale of goods without making technical changes to the goods;

2.Conduct distribution trade, so as to be the last liaison to deliver goods directly to consumers;

3.Conduct a collection trade, ie buying goods from small producers to be passed on to the intermediaries;

That:

a.Not owned by Indonesian citizens,

b.Legal entity or other legal entity, of which one or more of its shareholders or participants is not a citizen of Indonesia, with the understanding that companies that provide services by receiving instruction are exempt from the above provisions.

SECTION II:

LIQUIDATION OF FOREIGN SMALL/RETAIL TRADING COMPANIES

Article 2

Foreign small and retail trading companies that are banned based on the Decree of the Minister of Trade dated 14 May 1959 No. 2933/M must be closed no later than January 1, 1960, with the stipulation:

1.That from the date of enforcement of this Presidential Regulation, steps must be taken towards the liquidation of said companies;

2.That such provision does not mean that the foreigners concerned must leave their places of residence, unless prescribed by Regional Leadership in connection with the security situation.

Article 3

The companies referred to in Article 2 shall be compensated, taking into account local customs, with an amount determined by a committee, formed by the relevant Head of Region II (Regent) and consisting of the relevant Wedana-Assistant as chairman, local BODM and persons appointed by the Domestic Trade Service of the Ministry of Trade and the Cooperative Service of the Ministry of Transmigration, Cooperatives and Rural Community Development or by regional agencies authorized by the two ministers as members.

Article 4

(1)The compensation referred to in Article 3 is given to the companies in Article 2 in the form of:

a.Cash or

b.Loan.

(2)The amount of cash and loans in paragraph (1) of this Article is stipulated by taking into account the capital of the company in Article 2, both in the form of money, as well as goods, buildings and other property, which may be voluntarily used by the organization to continue the small business and retail trade.

(3)Loans referred to in paragraphs (1) and (2) of this article are allowed for a period not later than one year and with interest at a rate of 9% per annum, everything in accordance with the guidelines given by the Cooperative Office.

SECTION III:

TRANSFER OF RIGHTS AND PLACE OF FOREIGN SMALL/RETAIL TRADING COMPANIES

Article 5

The transfer of the rights of the companies referred to in Article 2 to national entrepreneurs or the transfer of small and retail trading locations by the companies referred to in Article 2 to a new location must be done with the permission of the Domestic Trade Service.

Article 6

Those who are allowed to receive the transfer of rights and those who are required to fill the small trading places and the vacancies referred to in Article 5 are the national entrepreneurs who organize their organizations on the basis of cooperatives.

Article 7

Efforts in the field of cooperatives to accommodate the jobs referred to in Article 6 are carried out in the following ways:

a.Use existing cooperatives;

b.Form new cooperatives where there is no cooperative;

c.Organize stalls/shops that already exist into cooperatives;

d.Hold a pilot project per-store in the district, which will eventually have to be organized by a cooperative.

Article 8

(1)If a location does not yet have a cooperative, then, while waiting for the formation of such organization, the Wedana-Assistant with the assistance of BODM will form a committee, consisting of the head of the village concerned as chairman and two or more villagers as members, to accept the transfer of rights and/or continue the small and medium trading business referred to in Articles 5 and 6.

(2)Immediately after the formation of a cooperative, the committee referred to in paragraph (1) of this article is to assign its work to the organization, while the committee itself is then dissolved by the relevant Wedana-Assistant.

Article 9

(1)The personnel of companies referred to in Article 2 which have been closed shall be involved as volunteer employees as much as possible in the local organizations referred to in Articles 6, 7 and 8.

(2)The accommodation of personnel referred to in paragraph (1) of this Article is to be carried out in a prudent manner by paying attention to aspects of humanity.

(3)In carrying out such efforts in the preceding articles of this article, acts or actions that could cloud the atmosphere in the areas concerned must be avoided.

Article 10

Large traders and intermediary traders are obliged to gradually stop before January 1, 1960 to stop the distribution of goods to the companies referred to in Article 2 and to transfer them to national entrepreneurs referred to in Articles 6, 7 and 8.

SECTION IV:

IMPLEMENTATION PROVISIONS

Article 11

(1)The Deputy Minister of Trade where necessary together with the Deputy Minister of Transmigration/Cooperatives/Rural Community Development shall regulate the further implementation of the provisions in this Presidential Regulation, and shall have the right to make special regulations for areas deemed necessary.

(2)The Government Information Agency shall provide the widest possible information in order to educate the people on the importance of conducting small and retail trading through cooperatives.

SECTION V:

CLOSING PROVISIONS

Article 12

This Presidential Regulation is called the “SMALL AND RETAIL TRADING REGULATIONS” or for short “P.P.K.E.”, effective on the day it was appointed and starting on 10 July 10 1959.

As public notice, the promulgation of this Presidential Regulation is placed in the State Gazette of the Republic of Indonesia.

Chairman of the Presidium Cabinet,

Deliberates:

1.That in the context of nation and character building of Indonesia, the process of assimilation of Indonesian citizens of “foreign descent” within the body of the Indonesian nation must be accelerated.

2.That changing the names of Indonesians of foreign descent with names that match the original Indonesian names will be able to encourage this assimilation effort.

3.That, therefore, for Indonesian citizens who still use Chinese names, who wish to change their name according to the original Indonesian name, it is necessary to be given the widest possible facilities by holding special procedures.

Considering:

Law Number 4 of 1961.

DECIDES:

To stipulate:

The name change regulations for Indonesian citizens who use Chinese names are as follows:

SECTION I: GENERAL REQUIREMENTS

Article 1

1.Indonesian citizens who still use Chinese personal names and surnames who wish to replace their names with that of Indonesian names may state their wishes in writing to the Head of the Level II Region or an appointed official.

2.The names chosen must not violate the customs of an area or be considered as a title, and must not violate the moral code;

3.The Regional Head or the appointed official on behalf of the Minister of Justice shall provide a receipt for the change of name in question.

4.Since the person concerned receives the letter of acceptance as mentioned in paragraph (3), he can use the new name.

5.If within a period of 3 months after he receives the letter of acceptance as referred to in paragraph (3) above, there is no objection or lawsuit against the use of the new name from anyone who is channeled through the relevant Head of Level II Region, then he may use that name and is deemed to have obtained permission from the Minister of Justice as referred to in Law No. 4 Year 1961.

SECTION II: PROCEDURE

Article 2

1.To accomodate the implementation of the name change in accordance with the above, a special procedure which deviates from the usual procedure for that period of time is held.

2.The name change according to this provision is carried out by filling out a statement in 3 (three) copies where the old full name, date and place of birth and address in addition to the new full name of the person intending to change the name must be stated.

3.Each statement letter must be accompanied by a proof of citizenship of the Republic of Indonesia concerned.

4.The name change of children under the age of 18 is carried out by their parents or guardians in the same statement or separate statement;

5.Assignment Letter is submitted to the local Regent/KDH or Mayor/KDH to be registered, given a list number and legalized;

6.One piece was sent by the Minister of Justice of the Republic of Indonesia in Djakarta through the Office of the Regent/Mayor of the relevant KDH. One piece of affidavit shall be immediately returned to the person concerned, to endeavor to amend the birth certificate and; if any, the marriage certificate at the relevant Civil Registry Office;

7.The registered statement letter is numbered and legalized by the relevant Regent or Mayor/KDH having proper legal force;

SECTION III: MISCELLANEOUS

Article 3

1.In implementing this decision, the relevant regents and mayors of the KDH must provide the lightest facilities to the name changers in order to streamline the procedure;

2.For administrative fees, more than Rp. 25,- (twenty five rupiahs) may not be charged for each Statement;

3.Matters that have not been regulated in this decision will be regulated later by the Minister of Home Affairs;

4.This decree is enforced on 1 January 1967 and ends on 31 March 1968.

ON

CHINESE RELIGIOUS BELIEFS AND CULTURAL TRADITIONS

We, the Office of the President of the Republic of Indonesia,

Deliberates:

That the Chinese religious beliefs and cultural traditions in Indonesia which are centered on their ancestral country, which in their manifestations can cause unnatural psychological, mental and moral influences on Indonesian citizens that they become obstacles to the assimilation process, need to be regulated and their functions determined in reasonable proportions.

Considering:

1.1945 Constitution Article 4 Paragraph 1 and Article 29;

2.MPRS Decree No. XXVII/MPRS/1966 Section III Article 7 and Elucidation of Article 1 Paragraph (a);

3.Cabinet Presidium Instruction No. 37/U/IN/6/1967;

4.Presidential Decree No. 171/1967. jo. 163 of 1966.

With instructions to:

1.Minister of Religion;

2.Minister of Home Affairs

3.All government agencies and instruments at the central and all regional levels

DECIDES:

To implement the following basic policies regarding Chinese religion, belief and customs as follows:

(1)Without compromising the guarantee of freedom to embrace religion and worship, Chinese worship procedures which have aspects of cultural affinity centered on their ancestral country must be carried out internally in family or individual relationships.

(2)Celebrations of Chinese religious and traditional festivals are not carried out ostentatiously in public, other than in family environment.

(3)The determination of the category of religious beliefs, as well as the implementation for Chinese religious practices, beliefs and customs are regulated by the Minister of Religion after hearing the considerations of the Attorney General (PAKEM).

(4)The security and control over the implementation of this basic policy is regulated by the Minister of Home Affairs together with the Attorney General.

(5)This Instruction shall be enforced on the day of stipulation.
